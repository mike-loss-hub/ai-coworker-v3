# Reusable Skill: Stakeholder Navigation

Patterns for managing multi-stakeholder dynamics. Developed through standards body coordination and grantee coalition management.

## The Pattern
- Before any multi-stakeholder meeting, pre-brief each party separately
- Frame shared work as "enabling" not "replacing" anyone's existing effort
- Let the person with the most credibility in the room present the controversial idea
- Technical purists and political operators need different framings of the same proposal
- Never commit resources on someone else's timeline in a group setting

## Relationship Archetypes Encountered
- **The Technical Purist** — respects data and rigor, dismissive of vague claims. Bring specifics.
- **The Institutional Diplomat** — careful with commitments, thinks in terms of what their board will approve. Don't push for immediate answers.
- **The Practitioner** — no patience for theory disconnected from reality. Lead with "here's what this means for your students/staff."
- **The Co-Funder** — wants to see evidence before committing. Demonstrate impact, don't pitch vision.
