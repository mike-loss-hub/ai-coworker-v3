# Decision: Standards Alignment Strategy — CLR/VC Convergence
- **Date:** 2026-04-22
- **Status:** Decided
- **Decided By:** Program Officer (me)

## Context
The education credential space has two competing approaches: 1EdTech's Comprehensive Learner Record (CLR) and W3C's Verifiable Credentials (VC). Our portfolio depends on both — several grantees need to work across both ecosystems. We need a strategy for the May 5 standards alignment meeting.

## Options Considered
1. **Advocate for CLR adoption of VC format** — Push 1EdTech to align CLR with VC data model. Pros: VC has broader adoption outside education. Cons: 1EdTech may resist external pressure, could damage relationship with Linda Zhang.
2. **Advocate for bridge specification** — Support TrustLayer's approach of a bridging trust framework. Pros: respects both communities' work, pragmatic. Cons: adds complexity, a third spec to maintain.
3. **Stay neutral, convene only** — Host the conversation without taking a position. Pros: maintains trust with both sides. Cons: may result in no progress, wastes convening opportunity.

## Decision
**Option 2: Support bridge specification approach.** Publicly support TrustLayer's trust bridge concept at the May 5 meeting. Frame it as enabling interoperability without requiring either community to abandon their work. Use our convening position to facilitate technical discussion, not to pick a winner.

## Rationale
- James Okafor's trust bridge approach is technically sound and politically viable
- Both Linda Zhang and David Kowalski have expressed openness to bridging (not replacement)
- This aligns with our portfolio thesis — we fund infrastructure, not standards body politics
- The bridge approach lets our grantees build without waiting for full convergence
