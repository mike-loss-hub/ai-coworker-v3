# Decision: LearnTrail Research Support After CTO Departure
- **Date:** 2026-04-15
- **Status:** Decided
- **Decided By:** Program Officer (me), with input from Patricia Williams (VP Programs)

## Context
LearnTrail's CTO Dr. Kevin Park departed in March 2026. He was the primary architect of the provenance graph system — the core technical deliverable. PI Elena Vasquez reported incomplete knowledge transfer. The grant is in Year 1 with $350K remaining to disburse.

## Options Considered
1. **Immediate grant termination** — Recover remaining funds, redirect to pipeline. Pros: clean, fast. Cons: loses 6 months of provenance work, damages relationship, sends bad signal to other grantees.
2. **90-day support runway** — Continue funding with architecture review gate at May 30. Pros: gives team a chance, shows we support grantees through challenges. Cons: may be throwing good money after bad if architecture is unrecoverable.
3. **Restructure grant scope** — Reduce to what remaining team can deliver. Pros: preserves some outcomes. Cons: the reduced scope may not be strategically valuable.

## Decision
**Option 2: 90-day support runway with hard gate.** Continue funding through May 30 architecture review. If review shows a viable path forward (either with simplified architecture or new technical hire), adjust milestones and continue. If not, shift to Option 3 (scope reduction) or Option 1 (termination).

## Rationale
- The provenance graph work is the only grant covering AI-summary tracing — losing it creates a pillar gap
- Elena is a capable PI who was transparent about the problem — that behavior should be rewarded
- 90 days is enough time to assess viability without excessive risk
- Patricia Williams agreed this is the right balance of support and accountability
