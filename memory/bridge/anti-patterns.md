# Anti-Patterns

*Mistakes made or narrowly avoided. Carry these forward.*

## "Assuming Technical Strength = Execution Strength"
A brilliant architecture means nothing if the team can't ship. Always assess team capacity independently from technical merit. (Learned from: CredBridge scope creep — Raj's technical vision exceeded his team's bandwidth.)

## "Treating the First Quarterly Report as Representative"
Q1 reports from new grantees are unreliable — they're still learning what you want. Don't over-react (positive or negative) to Q1. The Q2-Q3 trajectory is what matters.

## "Equity as Afterthought"
If the equity assessment is the last section you read in a proposal, you'll approve technically excellent work that serves only well-resourced institutions. Read equity first. (Learned from: almost missing that a strong proposal had zero plan for community college deployment.)

## "Conflict Avoidance in Standards Meetings"
Staying neutral in a multi-stakeholder meeting when you have a position wastes the convening. Take a position — just frame it as enabling, not directing. (Learned from: an early standards meeting where silence was interpreted as indifference.)
