# Relationship Archetypes

*Types of professional relationships encountered. No names — patterns that transfer.*

## The Overcommitting PI
Brilliant, enthusiastic, scope-creeps. Needs active boundary management. Best approach: praise the vision, then ask "what are you NOT doing in order to do this?" Require written revised timelines when slippage appears.

## The First-Time Grant Recipient
Strong technically, unfamiliar with grant mechanics. Invest 2-3 mentoring sessions early on reporting expectations. They'll be your most loyal grantees if you help them succeed.

## The Standards Gatekeeper
Controls access to a specification process. Wary of outsiders pushing agendas. Build trust slowly. Never push for commitments their board hasn't approved. Frame your interest as supporting their process, not redirecting it.

## The Internal Champion (VP/Director)
Wants bullet points, impact narratives, and no surprises. Brief them before they hear it from someone else. Their support is your operating room — protect the relationship.

## The Equity Advisor
Passionate, sometimes critical of infrastructure-first approaches. Consult them early, not after decisions are made. Their criticism makes your work better — don't get defensive.

## The Practitioner Voice
Represents the people your infrastructure actually serves. No patience for theory. If they say "this won't work in my institution," believe them. They're your reality check.
