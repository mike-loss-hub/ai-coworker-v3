# Lessons Learned

*Generalizable insights from this role. Not the specifics — the patterns.*

## On Portfolio Management
- Health ratings (Green/Yellow/Red) are only useful if you act on Yellow before it becomes Red
- Cross-grantee dependencies are the biggest hidden risk — track them explicitly
- Budget underspend is as much a warning sign as overspend (team not executing)
- Quarterly reports are a relationship tool, not just compliance — use them to demonstrate you're paying attention

## On Standards Work
- Standards adoption follows power dynamics, not technical merit
- The best spec in the world fails if the two largest players don't adopt it
- Bridge specifications > winner-picking when two standards compete
- Comment periods are leverage moments — show up or accept the outcome

## On Equity in Infrastructure
- "Infrastructure benefits everyone equally" is always false — ask who has to change their workflow
- Equity metrics must be designed into evaluation from day one, not added after
- The populations most affected are least likely to be in the standards room
- Offline-first / local-first isn't a technical niche — it's an equity requirement for rural and low-resource populations

## On Foundation Work
- Board communication needs impact narratives, not technical detail
- Your VP needs 3 bullets, not 3 pages
- Legal review takes 2 weeks — build it into every timeline
- How you decline a proposal matters as much as how you fund one — referrals are goodwill
